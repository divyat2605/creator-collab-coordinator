BRAND–CREATOR COLLABORATION FRAMEWORK
====================================

This framework is a practical, opinionated guide for how brands and creators
work together inside the Collaboration Ledger. It replaces insurance “policy”
language with campaign-ready guidelines that are still structured, explicit,
and machine-readable.

The goal is simple:

    Make it obvious when a collaboration is a GREAT fit,
    when it should be fast-tracked,
    and when it needs a human in the loop.

The Collaboration Ledger turns these guidelines into structured events so that
the Advisor Agent, Match Agent, and Campaign Coordinator can reason about
brand risk, creator fit, expected performance, and operational effort.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1 — CREATOR TIERS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Creators are grouped into standardized tiers based on audience size and
baseline engagement. These tiers are descriptive, not prescriptive; they help
brands align budgets, expectations, and required review rigor.

Tier A — Emerging Specialists
-----------------------------
- Audience: 10K–100K followers / subscribers
- Typical engagement: 5–12%
- Positioning: Deeply trusted within a niche, high community intimacy
- Strengths:
  - High comment quality and direct creator replies
  - Flexibility to experiment with new formats
  - Efficient cost per engaged viewer
- Best used for:
  - Early product validation
  - High-signal feedback loops
  - Seeding stories, Reels, shorts, or live sessions

Tier B — Established Voices
---------------------------
- Audience: 100K–500K followers / subscribers
- Typical engagement: 3–8%
- Positioning: Recognized authority with consistent reach and repeat viewers
- Strengths:
  - Reliable traffic to key messages and landing pages
  - Strong storytelling and narrative arcs across content
  - Suitable for campaign anchors and hero pieces
- Best used for:
  - Product launches
  - Brand repositioning moments
  - Educational explainers that must travel beyond the core community

Tier C — Flagship Partners
--------------------------
- Audience: 500K+ followers / subscribers
- Typical engagement: 2–5%
- Positioning: Category-defining voices with cross-platform visibility
- Strengths:
  - Major reach and social proof
  - Ability to normalize new behaviors or categories at scale
  - High leverage for distribution of brand narratives
- Best used for:
  - Tentpole campaigns
  - New market entry
  - Competitive repositioning with clear “category story”

The Collaboration Ledger stores creator tier, engagement baselines, and past
campaign performance so that the Match Agent can reason about fit relative to
brand goals and risk appetite.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2 — ENGAGEMENT THRESHOLDS AND QUALITY SIGNALS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Advisor Agent and Match Agent never look at a single vanity metric in
isolation. Instead, they evaluate a bundle of signals that together indicate
whether a creator is likely to deliver meaningful outcomes for a specific
campaign.

Core quantitative thresholds
----------------------------
- Baseline engagement:
  - Tier A: 5%+ average engagement on recent sponsored and organic posts
  - Tier B: 4%+ on recent content, 3%+ on sponsored posts
  - Tier C: 3%+ on recent content, 2%+ on sponsored posts
- Completion or watch time:
  - Long-form video (10+ minutes): 50%+ typical completion, 65%+ considered
    strong
  - Short-form video (< 90 seconds): 65%+ typical completion, 75%+ considered
    strong
- Returning viewer / repeat viewer share:
  - 40%+ returning viewers over 90 days indicates a loyal, habit-driven
    audience, suitable for complex or educational products.

Qualitative engagement quality
------------------------------
- High quality comments:
  - Questions about how to use the product category
  - Screenshots or photos of viewers trying recommendations
  - Context-rich feedback (“this solved X for my team”, “I finally understood Y”)
- Low quality comments:
  - “First”, “🔥🔥🔥”, generic emojis with no content
  - Bots or unrelated spam threads
  - Disconnected meme chains not anchored to the topic

The Collaboration Ledger records both quantitative thresholds and qualitative
examples so that the Match Agent can explain WHY a collaboration meets or
fails guidelines, not just whether it passes.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3 — BRAND SAFETY AND VALUE ALIGNMENT
----------------------------------------------------------------------

This section replaces traditional “exclusion criteria” from insurance with
clear guidelines about brand safety, ethical standards, and values fit.

Baseline guidelines
-------------------
- No active involvement in hate, harassment, or dehumanizing content.
- No promotion of illegal products or services.
- No persistent pattern of misinformation in the creator’s primary domain.
- Compliance with platform policies and local advertising regulations.

Values and positioning fit
--------------------------
- Sustainability claims:
  - Creators discussing sustainability should demonstrate basic literacy about
    certifications, materials, or lifecycle impact.
  - The brand must avoid asking creators to overstate or misrepresent
    sustainability attributes.
- Equity and representation:
  - Collaborations in sensitive categories (health, finance, employment,
    education) should reflect diverse identities and avoid tokenism.
  - Creators should have a documented track record of speaking about these
    topics respectfully and thoughtfully.

The Advisor Agent highlights possible misalignments early, while the Match
Agent uses this context to either fast-track approval or require human review.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4 — FAST-TRACK COLLABORATION CLAUSES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Certain combinations of creator profile, past performance, and campaign type
qualify for fast-track approval. These function like “exception clauses” in
the previous insurance domain, but now they are positive signals that allow
the system to move faster with confidence.

Fast-Track Clause 4.1 — Returning Partner With Proven Performance
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Conditions:
- The creator has completed at least 2 prior campaigns with the brand.
- Post-campaign surveys or metrics show that agreed KPIs were met or exceeded.
- No major brand safety incidents or contract disputes occurred.

Effect:
- Skip manual review for standard campaign briefs of similar size and scope.
- Auto-approve within the Collaboration Ledger, with a note that the decision
  relies on historical performance.

Fast-Track Clause 4.2 — High-Engagement Niche Educator
------------------------------------------------------
Conditions:
- Tier A or Tier B creator with 6%+ average engagement in the last 90 days.
- Content is primarily educational or “how-to” within the brand’s category.
- Audience questions in comments align closely with brand’s ideal customer
  profile.

Effect:
- For education-led campaigns (tutorials, explainers, deep dives), the Match
  Agent may auto-approve with pre-defined guardrails:
  - Required disclosure language
  - Required links to documentation or help centers
  - Optional human review for scripts exceeding a specified budget threshold

Fast-Track Clause 4.3 — Safety-Conscious Sustainability Advocates
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Conditions:
- Creator works primarily in sustainability, climate, or ethical consumption.
- Past content shows a documented pattern of calling out greenwashing.
- Brand claims are verifiable and mapped to third-party standards or audits.

Effect:
- Campaigns focused on sustainable lines, recycled materials, or circular
  programs can be accelerated, with the Advisor Agent explicitly checking that
  all sustainability statements are backed by citations from the brand.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5 — MATCHING LOGIC AND PATHWAYS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Campaign Coordinator and Match Agent translate campaign intent and creator
profile into a clear pathway outcome. Outcomes are intentionally simple:

- MATCHED — strong fit, low risk, proceed as proposed.
- CONDITIONAL MATCH — promising fit, but requires adjustments or safeguards.
- DECLINED — not an appropriate fit based on current guidelines.
- PENDING REVIEW — requires human involvement (e.g., edge cases or new
  markets).

Key decision dimensions
-----------------------
- Audience fit:
  - Does the creator reach the audience segments the brand cares about?
  - Do the audience’s behaviors match the campaign’s call-to-action?
- Content format fit:
  - Does the creator habitually ship the formats proposed (e.g., Reels, shorts,
    carousels, live streams, long-form)?
  - Are there clear examples of similar content performing well?
- Brand safety and values:
  - Any red flags in past content?
  - Any historical conflicts with similar brands or categories?
- Operational complexity:
  - Number of deliverables and platforms involved.
  - Need for legal review, regulatory review, or product approvals.

The Collaboration Ledger stores each of these decision factors as structured
fields so that humans can later audit why a match was made.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6 — HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP SAFEGUARDS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Not every collaboration should be automated. The framework explicitly defines
which scenarios MUST route to human review.

Requires human review if:
-------------------------
- The creator or brand is operating in a heavily regulated space (healthcare,
  finance, employment, or education with high-stakes outcomes).
- The campaign involves sensitive topics such as mental health, identity,
  politics, or major life decisions.
- The Match Agent detects inconsistencies between brand claims and third-party
  evidence surfaced by the Advisor Agent.
- The requested content format or timeline is unprecedented for this creator.

In these cases, the Collaboration Ledger records:
- What triggered the “PENDING REVIEW” status
- Which clauses or thresholds were partially met or violated
- Suggested questions or checks for the human reviewer

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7 — MEASUREMENT, FEEDBACK, AND ITERATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The framework assumes every collaboration generates feedback that should shape
future matches.

Post-campaign data to log
-------------------------
- Reach and impressions across platforms
- Engagement rate compared to creator’s baseline
- Click-through, sign-up, purchase, or other downstream metrics
- Qualitative feedback from both brand and creator
- Any brand safety or alignment issues

The Collaboration Ledger allows the Advisor Agent to use this history as
context for future recommendations, and the Match Agent can:
- Automatically downgrade or upgrade creators within tiers.
- Tighten or relax fast-track clauses for specific categories.
- Suggest new collaboration patterns (e.g., converting a one-off into a
  recurring series if performance was outsized).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8 — SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------------

This collaboration framework encodes the rules that human partnership,
marketing, and legal teams already use every day—but in a way that is explicit
enough for AI agents to reason about.

Instead of asking, “Is this creator good?”, the system asks:
- “Good for which campaign?”
- “Under what constraints?”
- “With which safety nets?”

The result is a brand–creator matching workflow that is faster, safer, and
more transparent than ad-hoc, spreadsheet-driven decision making—without
removing humans from the loop where they matter most.
